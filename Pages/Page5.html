Additional Resources

These are the resources I would recommend if you wish to study Japanese.

  • Textbooks
    • Genki <-- this series has additional graded readers you can purchase (though they are a bit pricy). link to purchase Genki textbook
  • Kanji
    • visit wanikani.com --> this site offers three free levels for you to try their kanji learning method.
    • visit site with handwriting sheets --> this free resource contains tons of different practice sheets for kanji - you can even pick whether you want them sorted by grade level, JLPT level, and so on.
  • Reading (manga)
    • Chiisana Mori no Ookami-chan (The Wolf of the Small Forest): a simple, slife-of-life story about a wolf and her forest friends. It's a very easy read, and the story is adorable. I love how they included the Japanese culture in their lives as well.
    • Shoujo Shuumatsu Ryokou (Girls' Last Tour): a post-apocalyptic slice-of-life story that focuses on the travels of two young girls, Chito and Yuuri. This is one of my very favorite series, I loved it so much. It's silly and sad, and very peaceful to read about their journey and life.

    Here's a list of free resources:

    • visit imabi.org <-- a free grammar resource containing lots of different grammar explanations.
    • visit tadoku.org <-- this site has tons of free reading material known as "graded readers". They are stories written for adult learners but use basic language and limited vocabulary.
    • visit learnnatively.com <-- on this site you can browse books and manga/such by their approximate JLPT level. They also have a book club on there that you can check out.
    • visit wanikani book club <-- this community reads manga, children's books, novels, and more. They discuss them by each chapter and have tons of vocabulary lists prepared so it's easy to find a book you're interested in and start reading.
